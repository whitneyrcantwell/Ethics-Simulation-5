Your Situation
Three Months In

You are Jordan Reyes, a junior analyst at Meridian Financial Partners, a mid-sized wealth management firm. You've been here three months. The job is everything you worked for in school — finally.

Your direct supervisor, Dana Marsh, has been tough but fair. Her boss — Senior Vice President Marcus Chen — barely knows your name. Two other senior analysts, Kevin Taft and Priya Sundaram, have been friendly and occasionally helpful.

It's 6:47 PM on a Tuesday. Almost everyone has gone home. You stayed late to finish a model. A new email arrives — a forwarded message, sent to you by mistake. The original was meant for Kevin, not Jordan.

You open it.

FROM: p.sundaram@meridianfp.com
TO: j.reyes@meridianfp.com [SENT IN ERROR — intended for k.taft]
SUBJECT: FWD: Atlas acquisition — before Thursday
---FWD---
Kevin — saw you talked to the contact. If the Atlas deal closes Thursday like they're saying, our window is Tuesday/Wednesday. NDA doesn't cover us technically. Confirm you're in?

—P

You stare at the screen. You know Atlas Analytics is a client. You know "window" and "NDA" and "acquisition" in the same sentence means something. You just don't know exactly what to do with the fact that you now know it.

Your first instinct is:

The Next Morning

Wednesday. Kevin and Priya are already at their desks when you arrive. They're talking quietly. When you walk in, they both look up at you — and there's a half-second of something in that look. Then they're back to normal.

Did Priya realize she sent that email to the wrong Jordan? Does Kevin know? You don't know.

By 10 AM, you've confirmed through public sources that Atlas Analytics is, in fact, in acquisition talks — but the deal hasn't been announced publicly. That's all you know.

Your supervisor Dana emails you to ask if you have a minute to go over your quarterly numbers later. Routine stuff. She doesn't mention the email.

Decision 3 of 5
Priya Finds You

Late Wednesday afternoon, Priya appears at your desk. She glances around, then leans in.

"Hey — about that email. Wrong Jordan. I'm sure you figured that out. The content — it's complicated. There's context you don't have. It's not what it looks like."

She pauses. "I'd appreciate it if this stayed between us. We've been good to you here, Jordan. Kevin and I have gone to bat for you more than once in ways you don't even know about. This would really complicate things for people who've been in your corner."

She gives you a meaningful look. Then she walks away.

How do you process what just happened?

Decision 4 of 5
Thursday Morning

Thursday. 9:15 AM. Atlas Analytics announces it has been acquired by a larger firm — Apex Capital Group — in a deal valued at $340 million. The announcement hits financial news immediately.

You watch Kevin and Priya from across the floor. They are very calm. Kevin is on a call. Priya is typing.

You don't know if they traded. You don't know if it would show up anywhere. You don't know what happens next.

But you know what you read on Tuesday night. And you know that Priya asked you to keep quiet.

Your phone buzzes. It's your college friend who works at the SEC as a junior examiner. She texted you about the Atlas deal: "big one today — our team is going to be busy." Pure coincidence. She doesn't know you know anything.
